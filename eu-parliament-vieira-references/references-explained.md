> Thank you very much, chair.

> I'm not usually in any of these committees, but I thought to come for the side quest.

Generale gaming reference: [Side Quest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quest_(video_games)#Sidequest)

Interpretation: This is not my main job, but I'll do it anyway.

> It is dangerous to go alone into the digital future without clear consumer protections.

Gaming Reference to the [first Zelda game](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Legend_of_Zelda_(video_game)).
The first person the player encounters gives them a sword because "It's dangerous to go alone! Take this."

> And I don't want to set the world on fire, but the truth is that the way we own media has shifted into something that's very sus.

Gaming reference to Fallout games where the song "I Don't Want to Set the World on Fire" by the Inkspots (1941) can be heard in an intro or the in-game radio.

> Too often, citizens buy a game only to find out that the cake is a lie.

Gaming Reference to [Portal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portal_(video_game)) where the player is promised cake, but has to find out that someone who came before them learned that "the cake is a lie!".
It is written as graffity on walls that the main character is not supposed to see.

> You buy a game you hope to cherish for years, but then one day it's suddenly gone.

"gone" may refer to the song [Want You Gone](https://theportalwiki.com/wiki/Want_You_Gone) which plays at the end of [Portal 2].

> You think, "Shit, here we go again."

Gaming Reference to [GTA San Andreas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grand_Theft_Auto:_San_Andreas) where the protagonist says it in frustration.


> When a publisher pulls the plug, we realize that our princess is in another castle.

Gaming Reference to [Super Mario Bros](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Super_Mario_Bros.) where after some boss fight the player is told "Thank you Mario! But our princess is in another castle!".
This tells the player that the game is not finished yet.

> Our liberty, our libraries are emptied.

Not a gaming reference, but like a reference to the [Empty Library memorial](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Empty_Library).

> It's like we've died of dysentery.

Gaming reference to the game [The Oregon Trail](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oregon_Trail) where playable characters can die from the disease.

> As we've heard, currently players are forced to see the purchases that they made completely vanish.

> We should not have to endure and survive a marketplace where our purchases, which are often masterpieces and they bring so much joy, are treated as disposable.

> We cannot allow digital preservation to take an arrow to the knee.

Gaming Reference to the game [The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Elder_Scrolls_V:_Skyrim) where a non-playable character stopped his adventerous life because of this knee injury.

> So, a petition was started to address all of these issues and it is super effective.

Gaming Reference to the game (and series) [Pokémon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pok%C3%A9mon_Red,_Blue,_and_Yellow).
In the game the effectiveness of attacks varies by different factors. If an attack is particularly good - like water against fire - the game calls it "super effective".

> Uh, let us um therefore congratulate over 1 million citizens who signed it with one clear message to the EU.

> Don't be sorry, be better.

Gaming Reference to the game [God of War]() where Kratos says it to his son Atreus.


> It's time to finish the fight.

Gaming Reference to the Song [Finish the Fight](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qJq-F51QblI) from Halo.


> We must ensure that even when companies move on, the virtual worlds we paid for remains still alive.

Gaming Reference to the Song "Still Alive" from [Portal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portal_(video_game)) where the evil AI sings about how it is "still alive" after the player defeated it.


> Let us praise the sun on a new era of digital rights so that for the gamer of the future, it isn't simply game over.

Gaming Reference to the gesture of "praising the sun" in the game [Dark Souls](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dark_Souls).

> If some colleagues think that I've lost my mind a little bit, these were all uh video game references.

> I just wanted to thank uh the petitioners and say that the Green CFA group stands here in support
> and hope that the commission takes seriously these very practical suggestions that you brought today.

> Thank you.
